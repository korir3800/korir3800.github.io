Asteroids

Asteroids are remnants from the early Solar System formation, offering clues about its history. The majority are located in the asteroid belt.

  • Ceres: The largest asteroid and a dwarf planet.
  • Vesta: One of the largest asteroids, known for its bright surface.
  • Pallas: A large asteroid with an irregular shape.

Comets

Comets are often called "dirty snowballs" due to their composition of ice and dust. Their spectacular tails are visible when they approach the Sun.

  • Halley's Comet: The most famous comet, visible from Earth every 76 years.
  • Comet Hale-Bopp: One of the brightest comets of the 20th century.
  • Comet NEOWISE: A recent comet visible to the naked eye in 2020.

Meteoroids

Meteoroids vary in size and become meteors or meteorites depending on their interaction with Earth's atmosphere.

Type Description
Asteroid Small rocky body orbiting the Sun.
Comet Icy body that releases gas and dust, forming a coma and tail.
Meteoroid Small particle from a comet or asteroid.
Meteor Meteoroid that enters Earth's atmosphere, creating a streak of light.
Meteorite Meteoroid that survives passage through the atmosphere and impacts Earth's surface.

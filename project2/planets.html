Inner Planets

The inner planets are characterized by their rocky surfaces and are located closest to the Sun. They have few or no moons and lack ring systems.

  • Mercury: The smallest planet, known for its extreme temperatures and cratered surface.
  • Venus: Similar in size to Earth but with a thick, toxic atmosphere and scorching surface temperatures.
  • Earth: The only planet known to support life, with vast oceans and a protective atmosphere.
  • Mars: Known as the Red Planet, with evidence of past water flow and potential for future exploration.

Outer Planets

The outer planets are massive, lack solid surfaces, and are surrounded by rings and numerous moons. They are primarily composed of gases and ices.

  • Jupiter: The largest planet, famous for its Great Red Spot and many moons.
  • Saturn: Known for its prominent ring system and diverse moons.
  • Uranus: An ice giant with a unique sideways rotation and faint rings.
  • Neptune: The furthest planet, characterized by its intense blue color and supersonic winds.

The Kuiper Belt

The Kuiper Belt is a region beyond Neptune that contains icy bodies, dwarf planets, and comets. It extends from about 30 AU to 50 AU from the Sun and is home to objects like Pluto and Haumea.

The Oort Cloud

The Oort Cloud is a hypothetical, distant region of icy objects that is believed to surround the Solar System, far beyond the Kuiper Belt. It is thought to be the source of long-period comets.

  • Location: The Oort Cloud is thought to extend from about 50,000 AU to as far as 100,000 AU from the Sun.
  • Origin of Comets: Long-period comets are believed to originate from the Oort Cloud when disturbed by nearby stars or galactic forces.

Comets and Their Origins

Comets are icy bodies that originate from the Kuiper Belt and Oort Cloud. As they approach the Sun, they develop tails of gas and dust, creating the spectacular displays we see from Earth.

  • Comet Halley: The most famous periodic comet, visible from Earth approximately once every 76 years.
  • Comet NEOWISE: A bright comet visible to the naked eye in 2020.

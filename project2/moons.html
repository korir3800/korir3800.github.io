Earth's Moon

Our Moon is the fifth largest in the Solar System and plays a crucial role in Earth's tides and climate. It has been a constant companion to Earth for over 4 billion years.

Jupiter's Moons

Jupiter's moons are a diverse group, ranging from volcanic Io to icy Europa, which may harbor a subsurface ocean.

  • Io: The most volcanically active body in the Solar System.
  • Europa: Possesses a subsurface ocean beneath its icy crust.
  • Ganymede: The largest moon in the Solar System, larger than Mercury.
  • Callisto: Heavily cratered and ancient surface.

Saturn's Moons

Saturn has a diverse collection of moons, each with unique characteristics, from the hazy Titan to the icy Enceladus.
